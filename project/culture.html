A mixed place for sure!

Curitiba was not only colonized by diverse peoples with unique cultures, but has also been the home of thousands of people from all over.

Curitiba, the capital of Paraná in Brazil, is a city renowned for its rich cultural diversity, shaped significantly by waves of immigration. The city's cultural fabric is a vibrant tapestry woven by various ethnic groups, including Italian, Polish, German, Ukrainian, and Japanese communities, among others. Each group has left an indelible mark on Curitiba's identity, from its architecture and cuisine to its festivals and traditions.

The influence of European colonization is particularly evident in the city's neighborhoods and cultural institutions. The Santa Felicidade district, for example, is a hub of Italian heritage, offering authentic Italian restaurants and cultural events. Similarly, the Polish and Ukrainian communities have preserved their traditions through churches, folk dance groups, and festivals, such as the famous Polish-inspired "Feira do Polonês."
